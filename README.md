## Project Overview

The **Carroll Tech and Innovation Council**, in partnership with the Carroll County Sheriff's Office, runs a monthly public workshop series called **Smart Steps: Digital Safety**. I was brought on to assess the program's existing curriculum, identify gaps, update outdated content, and create new training materials and multimedia resources to improve engagement and reach.

### Key Responsibilities

- Conducted a formal evaluation of existing training materials against current cybersecurity best practices
- Identified content gaps and areas for improvement through stakeholder meetings and in-person workshop observation
- Rebuilt and enhanced existing presentations for accessibility and clarity
- Developed two fully original training presentations on new topics
- Created three Canva infographics optimized for social media, handouts, and web distribution
- Produced an animated instructional video using PowToon
- Delivered all finalized materials to CTIC and secured an ongoing volunteer presenter role

## Challenges and Solutions

One significant challenge was low and zero workshop attendance at both observation sessions, which limited my ability to gather direct audience feedback to inform content decisions. Rather than treat this as a blocker, I reframed the solution: by expanding content delivery **beyond the workshop format**, I could help CTIC reach community members where they already are. This led to a stronger focus on social-media-ready infographics and a shareable instructional video — resources that extend the program's reach regardless of in-person attendance.

This experience reinforced a core GRC principle: when one control fails, compensating controls should be designed and implemented.
